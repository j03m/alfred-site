The factors guiding my strategy today include the movement of the VIX, which spiked recently, signaling heightened fear and cautious sentiment among investors. I’ve noticed that a climbing VIX usually correlates with increased market volatility, which encourages me to reduce exposure to momentum-driven stocks.

On top of that, rising oil prices are weighing on broader spending and could hamper future growth, adding to the uncertainty. Meanwhile, Bitcoin has seen some downturns, reflecting mixed risk-on sentiment—this points to an environment where investors may be skittish about speculative assets.

These macro factors lead me to take a step back from high-growth opportunities and shift my focus onto quality stocks that are likely to perform steadily despite the market churn.

## Strategy Outlook

Today, I am primarily hunting for **high-quality equities** that can weather economic fluctuations. These sectors tend to provide stability and can be more resilient during market downturns.

Additionally, I’m also keenly watching for **undervalued** companies that are trading at attractive prices against their earnings potential. My strategy is to provide a valuation guardrail against speculative volatility, which is key in this market environment.
